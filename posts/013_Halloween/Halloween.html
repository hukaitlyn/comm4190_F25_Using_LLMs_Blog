Can AI Do Halloween Better Than Me?

I handed over my Halloween costume decision to an LLM and hoped for the best. Here are the top picks and my reactions.
LLMs
Halloween
Author

Kaitlyn Hu

Published

October 20, 2025

Introduction:

Halloween and I have a long history of doing the absolute minimum. Most years I panic grab something from my closet and hope no one notices I put in zero effort. But since it is my senior year, I wanted to at least try a little harder. Not full Pinterest dedication, just better than usual. My goal was to reuse as much as possible from my closet while still looking cute, fun, and a little creative.

Instead of spending hours scrolling or overthinking it, I decided to let an LLM choose my Halloween costume for me. I wanted to see if AI could actually understand my vibe and give me ideas I would genuinely wear. Nothing expensive, nothing uncomfortable, just realistic options that match my style. I was not sure if it would be helpful or chaotic, but it made the whole decision feel more fun and a lot less stressful.

Prompt:

I need help choosing my Halloween costume this year. I am a college senior and I want to try a little harder than usual while still using as much as I can from my own closet. I want something cute, fun, and creative but still realistic and comfortable to wear. Ask me three short questions first to understand my style, personality, comfort level, and what I might already own. After I answer, give me at least five costume ideas that genuinely fit my vibe. Explain why each idea matches me and how easy it would be to put together with common clothes and accessories.

Getting To Know My Style:

ASSISTANT: Okay, I can help you brainstorm some costume ideas that fit your style. Answer these three quick questions.

  1. Describe your everyday style in three words. What colors do you usually wear
  2. On a scale from 1 to 5, how much effort are you willing to put into a costume, and are you okay with simple makeup or props
  3. Is there a theme, aesthetic, or show you have been drawn to lately You can keep it general

I will give you personalized ideas after your answers.

USER:

  1. comfy, trying to be trendy, mostly neutrals like black, white, grey, navy
  2. normally a three, but for Halloween a four
  3. anything cute and recognizable, nothing anime

Final Costume Reveals:

ASSISTANT: Here are five costume ideas that match what you told me. Each one uses your closet, fits your color palette, and is cute and recognizable.

1. Modern Wednesday Addams

Why it fits you: The black and white color palette matches your everyday style, and it is cute, trendy, and very recognizable.

How to make it: A black dress layered over a collared shirt, black tights, boots, simple braids, dark eyeliner.

2. Chic Vampire

Why it fits you: Works perfectly with your dark neutrals and gives you an elegant, cute, and slightly dramatic Halloween look.

How to make it: All black outfit, dark lipstick, simple fangs, optional fake blood.

3. Audrey Hepburn in Breakfast at Tiffany’s

Why it fits you: A timeless, classy costume that looks like you put in effort without being uncomfortable.

How to make it: Black dress, black flats or heels, pearls, sunglasses, hair up.

4. Modern Mime Artist

Why it fits you: Black and white aesthetic, cozy, creative, and instantly recognizable.

How to make it: Striped or plain shirt, black pants or skirt, light makeup with bold eyeliner, optional beret.

5. Taylor Swift Reputation Era

Why it fits you: Cute, trendy, and exactly your color palette. Recognizable without being over the top.

How to make it: All black outfit, dark makeup, boots, and snake-themed accessories.

Reflection:

Trying this experiment actually ended up being more helpful than I expected. I thought the AI would give me random or over-the-top ideas, but the suggestions were surprisingly on point. What made me laugh a little was how many of the characters it mentioned were ones I had already brainstormed myself at some point. Wednesday Addams, Audrey Hepburn, a cute vampire moment, even Reputation era Taylor Swift all fit the exact color palette I wear every day. It made me feel like the AI actually understood my style better than I expected.

I also appreciated that all the ideas were realistic and comfortable, which is exactly what I wanted. Nothing felt dramatic or out of character for me. Everything was cute, recognizable, and easy to build from pieces I already own. It felt like the AI filtered my entire closet and personality into a small list of options that made total sense.

Overall, I was pleasantly surprised by how well this worked. It made the whole process feel more fun and way less stressful, and it reminded me that my everyday style already leans toward a lot of Halloween-adjacent looks. I would definitely use AI again for costume brainstorming, especially when I want something low effort but still cute.
